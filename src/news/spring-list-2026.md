* Ian Estacio, one of our postdocs, recently submitted his paper, "Effect of urban greening on walking frequencies across socioeconomic groups: Exploratory insights from agent-based modeling". **About**: Urban greening may enhance the walkability of cities, ultimately reflecting a wide range of health, social, and economic benefits for residents. Yet, there is still a lack of studies showing how urban greening improves walking frequencies across socioeconomic groups. In this study, we developed TrecSim (Tree Effect on walking Comfort Simulator), an agent-based model for simulating the effect of tree planting on the walking frequencies of commuters across socioeconomic groups. Congratulations Ian!
