Our resources

Our lab is located at the University of Arizona’s richard a. harvill building, room 320. We count with the following resources.

Our conference room

Our conference room is designed to accommodate 7-8 individuals and is equipped with state-of-the-art technology for seamless meetings and gatherings. It features a Logitech MeetUp camera system integrated with a large TV, facilitating seamless broadcasting of gatherings and connectivity for online meetings through platforms such as Zoom.

Positioned nearby is a small library containing books pertinent to our research and topics relevant to academic success. Visitors are encouraged to peruse the collection or contribute by donating relevant literature. Additionally, the conference room boasts two desk stations designed with the latest furniture styles, catering to the needs of both students and employees.

Modern design Experience a professional and comfortable space for conferences and reunions.
Kitchenette area Adjacent to the conference room, enjoy access to a well-equipped kitchenette.

Study/work office

Our study office offers an additional comfy two desk space for study and work-related activities. It provides a quiet and focused environment separate from the conference room.

Explore our state-of-the-art computing resources

The spirit of the laboratory extends beyond the ua campus in Arizona, as we collaborate with researchers across the US and internationally. We utilize mattermost, an open-source communication platform similar to discord and slack, to communicate effectively, share resources, schedule meetings, and plan our projects with organization boards.

Lab cluster

Unparalleled computing performance

Our lab is equipped with a cutting-edge dell precision 7920 server, featuring dual intel xeon gold 5217 CPUs, an NVIDIA RTX A5000 (24GB GDDR6) graphics card, 192GB of DDR4 RAM, and a lightning-fast 4TB M.2 NVMe SSD. This powerful setup enables us to handle complex data processing tasks with ease.

UA HPC cluster

UA HPC cluster

We also have access to the University of Arizona's high performance computing (HPC) cluster. This system provides exceptional capabilities for high throughput computing, distributed memory parallel computing, and shared memory parallel computing. For detailed specifications and access information, please refer to the UA HPC website: https://rc.arizona.edu/hpc-htc/computingsystems

Data management

Data management

To securely store our research data for the long term, we utilize a synology DS723+ network attached storage (NAS) device. This provides a centralized and reliable data management solution.

Cutting-edge technology

Cutting-edge technology

The lab employs mattermost, a self-hosted, open-source communication platform, to facilitate efficient collaboration within our team and with researchers located around the world. Mattermost's features allow us to share resources, schedule meetings, and effectively manage projects through its integrated organization boards, streamlining our research processes regardless of geographical distances.
